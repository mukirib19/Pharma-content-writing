# pharma-content-writing

A repository of writing samples and guides focused on pharmaceutical and healthcare content. The work here is informed by direct professional experience working as a front desk professional at a pharmaceutical company — managing visitor compliance, handling sensitive documentation, coordinating between departments, and communicating regulatory requirements to a wide range of people every day.

That experience shapes everything about how I approach healthcare content. It is not academic knowledge of the industry — it is ground-level familiarity with how pharmaceutical environments actually operate, what patients and visitors genuinely struggle to understand, and where communication breaks down between clinical language and plain human speech.

---

## Background

Before transitioning into full-time content writing and AI content strategy, I worked as a front desk professional at a pharmaceutical company. The role involved:

- **Visitor and patient intake** — greeting, verifying, and processing visitors according to strict compliance protocols, including GMP and site access requirements
- **Document handling** — managing controlled documents, visitor logs, non-disclosure agreements, and departmental correspondence with accuracy and confidentiality
- **Cross-departmental communication** — acting as the first point of contact between external visitors, delivery personnel, regulatory inspectors, and internal teams including production, quality assurance, and HR
- **Compliance communication** — explaining site rules, hygiene protocols, and restricted area policies to visitors who had no pharmaceutical background, in language they could understand and act on immediately
- **Incident and access management** — handling access control issues, flagging compliance breaches, and escalating concerns through the correct internal channels

This experience gave me a direct understanding of the gap between how pharmaceutical companies communicate internally and how patients, visitors, and the general public actually receive and process that information. Closing that gap through clear, accurate writing is what this repository is about.

---

## What is inside this repository

- **patient-facing-content-guide.md** — a practical guide to writing patient-facing content in healthcare with clarity, accuracy, and respect for the reader. Draws directly on front desk experience to illustrate where communication typically fails and how to fix it.

More writing samples and guides will be added over time.
